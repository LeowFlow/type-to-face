About

Type-To-Face

Type-To-Face is an interactive typographic system that reconstructs the human face in real time using letterforms. A live webcam feed is sampled, translated, and rendered as a shifting field of type.

Concept

The project translates pixel data into typographic characters: glyph density, weight, kerning, and scale. Each sampled cell becomes a point where image information is interpreted through type.

Identity is not directly represented. It is reconstructed through a system of substitutions, where the face is shaped by the spacing, density, and structure of letterforms.

Typography is treated as structural material rather than language alone. Letters build tone, contour, rhythm, and surface, allowing the portrait to exist between reading and image-making.

Historical Context

Type-To-Face sits within a lineage of typographic image-making. Typewriter art, including Julius Nelson's Artyping, used mechanical characters as modular units for constructing images.

ASCII art and early internet culture carried this logic into screen space, using restricted character sets as a visual language of limitation, translation, and display.

Concrete poetry, particularly Apollinaire's calligrammatic work, connected the visual arrangement of language to meaning and form. These precedents inform the project by positioning letters as signs, structures, and image-making matter.

Contemporary Context

Contemporary generative design, especially the systems collected in Generative Gestaltung, provides a framework for thinking through rules, parameters, and visual output.

Type-To-Face extends those ideas through live input. The composition is not fixed in advance; it is continuously recalculated from the participant's face.

Outcome

The outcome is a web-based interactive application: webcam input is converted into a real-time typographic reconstruction.

The work is also designed for exhibition, using a projector and live participants to bring the typographic reconstruction into a shared physical space.
